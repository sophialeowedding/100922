Ulm

Visit the Ulmer Münster, the Fishermen’s Quarter and stroll by the Danube river. Ulm is founded in medieval times and the Ulmer Münster is currently the tallest church in the world.

   

Tübingen

Tübingen is a town which combines the flair of a lovingly restored medieval centre with the colorful bustle and typical atmosphere of a young and cosmopolitan students' town. With two castles just outside the city, Tübingen is definitely a place worth visit.

Stuttgart

Stuttgart, the capital of Baden-Wuerttemberg state, is known as a manufacturing hub. Mercedes-Benz and Porsche have headquarters and museums here. The city center is perfect for a shopping day or a night out.


Mercedes Benz Museum
Porsche Museum
   

Schloss Sigmaringen

Standing high on a cliff above the Danube river, Sigmaringen Castle was built as a fortress. Over the centuries, it has been transformed into a royal residential palace, complete with fairytale towers and turrets, bay windows and terraces.

   

Schloss Neuschwanstein

The ULTIMATE Disney Castle!
Neuschwanstein Castle is a 19th-century historicist palace on a rugged hill above the village of Hohenschwangau near Fuessen in southwest Bavaria, Germany. The palace was commissioned by King Ludwig II of Bavaria as a retreat and in honour of Richard Wagner.

   

Munich

Munich, Bavaria’s capital, is home to centuries-old buildings and numerous museums. The city is known for its annual Oktoberfest celebration and its beer halls, including the famed Hofbräuhaus.
